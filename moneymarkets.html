Money Markets

Money markets are a type of investment where short-term debt securities with high liquidity are bought and sold. They are considered low-risk investments and are often used by investors who want to preserve capital while earning some interest.

Here are some popular money market options:

  • Treasury Bills
  • Certificate Of Deposits
  • Commercial Paper
  • Repurchase Agreements
  • Money Market Funds

Investors can choose from various money market instruments based on their risk tolerance and investment goals.

Treasury Bills

Treasury bills are short-term government debt securities that mature in less than one year.

Certificate Of Deposits

Certificate of Deposits (CDs) are time deposits offered by banks and financial institutions.
